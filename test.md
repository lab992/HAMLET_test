# HAMLET
=======

**H**ierarchical **A**gent-based **M**arkets for **L**ocal **E**nergy **T**rading

## About
-------

HAMLET is an open-source tool for the agent-based development and testing of energy market applications at a local level. It provides a flexible and extensible framework for simulating various energy trading scenarios and analyzing their outcomes. This allows researchers and developers to explore the complexities of local energy markets and design more efficient and resilient systems.

## Description
------------

The increasing complexity of energy systems necessitates more detailed and realistic models. Agent-based modeling offers a promising approach by simulating the behavior of individual actors within these systems. HAMLET is a modular, extendable, and open-source toolbox developed to address this need, facilitating the development and testing of market designs with a focus on local interactions at the distribution level. It enables researchers to investigate the interplay between market participants and the impact of various market designs on the technical and economic performance of energy systems.

HAMLET's design prioritizes extendability and modularity, allowing for easy swapping of functionalities. This enables researchers to test alternative market clearing algorithms, trading strategies, and control strategies. The toolbox aims to provide a common platform for developing, testing, and comparing different market designs, fostering collaboration and facilitating reproducible research. Comprehensive documentation is currently under development and will be available soon.

HAMLET was developed and is maintained by the [Chair of Renewable and Sustainable Energy Systems](https://www.epe.ed.tum.de/en/ens/homepage/) of the [Technical University of Munich](https://www.tum.de/en/). Version 0.1 (February 2024) was developed as part of the research project [STROM](https://www.epe.ed.tum.de/en/ens/research/projects/current-projects/strom-sp-3/), funded by the Bavarian Ministry of Economic Affairs, Regional Development and Energy.
